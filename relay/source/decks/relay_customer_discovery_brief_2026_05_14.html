OUTREACH PRINCIPLES · HOW TO REACH OUT WITHOUT SELLING

Lead with their pain. Not our product.
Pretend you're not selling. Because you're not — you're learning.

Principle 1 · Pretend you're not selling

  • "Building something, want to hear your reality" beats "we have a product" 4:1
  • No demo. No deck. No pitch.
  • "Need 30 min of your reality" → high acceptance

Principle 2 · Lead with their pain

  • First message names their specific pain · not our category
  • "Saw you do COD — curious how you handle RTO right now" → opens doors
  • "Cashfree has a new product" → ignored

Principle 3 · Offer something asymmetric

  • Free Cashfree merchant benchmark report
  • Their RTO vs peer cohort (we have the data)
  • Early access waitlist for design partners

The exact one-page pitch · use this verbatim

Subject: Building something for [their pain] — need 30 min of your reality

Opening line: "I run developer ecosystem at Cashfree. Not here to sell anything. Building a workflow product for [D2C ops / fintech subscriptions / lending]. Want to understand how you handle [their pain] today and what's broken."

That's it. No deck. No demo. Listen for 28 minutes. Two minutes at the end to ask "would you try the beta if we built X?"

OUTPUT PER CALL · DISCOVERY TEMPLATE

Every call produces 5 artifacts.
Without these, the call didn't happen.

1 · Filled discovery template

  • 5 questions answered verbatim · their words, not yours
  • Direct quotes > paraphrase
  • Anchor each answer with a number where possible

2 · Pain heat-map

  • Rate 1–5 across the 5 use cases for that ICP
  • Forces priority across the cohort
  • Reveals the #1 wedge per ICP

3 · Workaround economy

  • ₹ spend today on the stitched workaround
  • Hours per week spent manually
  • The competing budget we're after

4 · Decision-maker map

  • Who else needs to approve trying a new tool?
  • Names · titles · their incentives
  • Path to design partner signature

5 · Design partner score

  • Would they commit to a beta with their name attached?
  • Yes · Maybe · No — with reasons
  • Feeds into the cohort selection

The synthesis Friday

  • Patterns across the week's calls
  • Update positioning doc with verbatim quotes
  • Adjust week 2 questions based on what we learned
  • Flag any ICP that's failing the validation test
