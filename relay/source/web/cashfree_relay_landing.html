workflow.yaml
wf_payments_19 · v 1.4
# Refund recovery workflow
name: refund-recovery
version: "1.4"
trigger:
  type: webhook
  event: payment.failed

steps:
  - id: filter_amount
    type: condition
    when: "$.amount > 500"

  - id: classify
    type: agent_skill
    skill: refund_triage
    threshold: 0.72
    on_below: manual_queue

  - id: enrich
    type: mcp
    server: hubspot
    tool: contact.lookup

  - id: compose_sms
    type: agent_skill
    skill: retry_message
    context: [classify, enrich]

  - id: send_link
    type: cashfree
    action: payment_link.create_and_send

policy:
  audit: on
  pii_redaction: strict
  region: ap-south-1

Common questions

Things our beta merchants asked first.

Is Relay live yet?

Private beta. Free for the businesses on it. Once we open it up, you pay for what you run, and you can see every action on the bill before it gets billed.

Will the agent move money without asking me?

Not unless you tell it to. Every skill has a confidence bar. Every step can wait for a human to approve. Anything the agent is unsure about lands in your team's queue.

Where does my data live?

In Indian data centers. Always. Card and Aadhaar data never reach the AI. Logs, runs, and embeddings stay inside the country. DPDP consent and erasure are part of the platform.

Do I need engineers to use this?

No. The whole product is built so a payment ops or finance ops lead can ship a workflow on her own. Engineers can dive in if they want a CLI or YAML. They do not need to.

How is this different from the Cashfree MCP server?

The MCP server lets external AI tools call Cashfree. Relay is the place where your team builds and runs workflows inside Cashfree. Most teams end up using both.

What happens if a workflow breaks at 2am?

It does not break silently. Every failure goes to a queue with the reason. Retries run on the schedule you set. Critical anomalies page the right person on Slack.
